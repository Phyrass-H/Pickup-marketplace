# 04 — PickUp · Business Model & Go-to-Market

> Scope: how PickUp makes money, the economics, the target market, the beta plan, and the central liquidity risk.
> **Last updated:** current session.

## Revenue model
- PickUp earns a **commission** as the intermediary (agent). Target structure (from the teaser): **~22.5% total**, split ~12.5% from Businesses and ~10% from Drivers — well below Uber's ~40%.
- The **Business** adds its own margin on top of the PickUp price and resells to its Guest with zero logistics overhead (it sees a capped/max price upfront via the ceiling).
- Per-trip economics (worked example, 15% illustrative commission) live in Doc 01.

## Pricing philosophy
- **Progressive Dynamic Pricing (PDP)** — a reverse-auction dynamic: fare starts underquoted and rises over time until a Driver accepts or it hits the Business's ceiling. Creates urgency/FOMO on the Driver side and fills missions. *Personally validated by the founder from his own driving experience.*
- Advance bookings don't need instant confirmation; **SPEED WIN** covers urgent last-minute trips with a high starting fare.
- The Business sets the ceiling; PickUp only recommends (see Doc 02 pricing mechanic + Doc 01 legal note).

## Target market & rollout
- **Beta:** French Riviera — Cannes, Nice + one more town. Airport transfers (Nice Côte d'Azur) for hotels is the natural first use case.
- **Then:** scale across the Riviera → Europe → globally.
- **Long-term distribution ambitions:** Amadeus GDS integration; potential public-sector transport contracts.

## Comparables
- **Blacklane, Wheely** — premium chauffeur platforms (named in the investor teaser).
- **Uber/Bolt** — mass ride-hailing; cautionary tale on agent-vs-principal VAT (Doc 01).
- **Booking.com** — agent/commission model reference.

## The #1 risk: liquidity (cold start)
A two-sided marketplace is worthless until both sides are populated in the same place at the same time.
- **Driver side:** ~secured (~200 lined up, briefed as a test).
- **Business side:** the open problem — needs someone well-connected in Riviera hospitality. **This, not engineering, is what makes or breaks the launch**, and a knowledgeable investor will probe it first.
- **Beta de-risking:** a human confirms every trip and reassures the Business the Driver will show up — a concierge safety layer on top of working software, suited to a cautious premium clientele. (A WhatsApp/spreadsheet approach was considered and rejected: fine for one mission, wrong for hundreds/week and damaging to credibility with premium hotels.)
